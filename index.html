WMT 2024 Machine Translation Project

Represented Stevens Institute of Technology in the prestigious WMT 2024 international NLP conference, specifically in the Spanish to Aragonese constrained translation task. The challenge required building a machine translation system using a model under 1 billion parameters, exclusively relying on data provided by WMT organizers.

For this project, I evaluated several models including MT5, MBERT, and NLLB, ultimately selecting NLLB for its superior BLEU score. Utilizing FairSequence, I developed a robust translation pipeline, enabling efficient testing and performance assessment of various models.

Given the low-resource nature of Aragonese, I implemented an iterative backtranslation technique. This involved generating a synthetic dataset by translating Spanish text into Aragonese and then using this synthetic data to fine-tune the model for forward translation. This process was repeated to progressively enhance the quality of the translations, ultimately improving the accuracy of the final model.

The final system achieved a BLEU score of 59.8, placing it among the top 10 in the competition (pending final results). There is potential for co-authoring a paper on the innovative techniques employed in this submission. Due to an NDA with my university, I cannot share the code or project details, but you can learn more about the competition by visiting the conference's page using the button below.
